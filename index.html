Dancehall is a genre of Jamaican popular music that originated in the late 1970s and can be regarded as a spin-off of Reggae. It can be exciting, it can be heated, it can be violent, it can be sexual, it can be fun. Always lyrically magical.
Mega-Wattage sound systems have blasted the electronically enhanced "riddims" and tongue-twisting lyrics of Jamaica's dancehall DJs across the globe. Of course, in such a competitive world everyone wants to be Number one and so the Sound Clash was born. Competitors from Jamaica, New York, Japan, London, Germany and even Egypt regularly take part in 'World Clashes'.



Sound Clashes are typically reserved for musical professionals and in front of large stage show crowds. Sound system DJ's battle against each other musically to become the 'Champion Sound', always determined by the Boos or Yays ('forwards') of the frenzied fans (and even more obviously, the throwing of bottles at crappy competitors).

I'd like to bring this experience into a more casual environment, enabling even the everyday music lover to prove his/her musical might, but on a global scale. Right from their couch.
Simply put, the platform will allow players around the world to compete by playing songs of their own selection. Winners and ratings will be 'crowd-sourced' from live listeners. The players stand to gain popularity, ratings and champion status. Listeners will be treated to hit after hit of their favourite music. Gratitude would only have to be shown through a vote.



Various avenues exist for monetization, including the tiered enablement of features for both players and listeners. Players will gain abilities to play longer clips, sound effects, branding jingles and the sort.
Listeners will not be restricted in any way whatsoever, as they are the source of the platform's success. Players need listeners.
Third parties may benefit from audio and graphical advertisements.
